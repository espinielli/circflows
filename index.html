Estimating extra-European flight flows

The flows above are about the extra-European flights for the years 2007-2014 which do not originate and arrive in one of the Eurocontrol member States' airports: internal flight flows have been filtered out because their much bigger size would otherwise mask the extra-European ones.
Furthermore military and helicopters flights have been taken out, as well as the flights whose departing (arriving) aerodrome is unknown (this last point looks like to be unconceaveable but it happens: flight plans have to be valid within its area of responsability for the Network Manager to accept them...)
This last set of filtered out flights do amount to very low figures and do not distort the overall distribution of the flows.
Finally, in order to clean the visualization and reduce the number of very small flows we have decided to show only the countries with more than 5 flights/day flows.

Please note that the grouping into regions is arbitrary. We have kept all Eurocontrol member States under the same group irrespective of their accession date.
The other regions are defined as follows:

Eurocontrol
Eurocontrol member States (Albania, Armenia, Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Georgia, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Moldova, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, The former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia, Turkey, Ukraine and United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland) plus Kosovo.
Middle East
definition as per Wikipedia minus Cyprus and Turkey which are grouped under Eurocontrol: Bahrain, Egypt, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Oman, Palestine, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Syria, United Arab Emirates and Yemen.
America
Northern, Central and Southern America countries plus Greenland and Iceland.
Africa
All countries in the continent but Egypt which is included in Middle East.
Asia
all the rest of the world.

How to read the plot

How to read the plot

Origins and destinations are represented by the circle’s segments. Each region/country is assigned a colour. Flows have the same colour as their origin and the width indicates their size. The direction of the flow is also shown by the gap between flow and country/region: small gap indicates origin; large gap indicates destination. Click on a segment to switch between country and region view.

Switch between time periods using Ctrl and number keys (1,2,3,4); reset the plot using Ctrl and r; zoom in/out with Ctrl and +/-
